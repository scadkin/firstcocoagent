# CSTA Chapter Partnership Sequence — Reference Template

*Strategy tag: `csta_partnership` | Priority tier: 620-719*

## Who this targets

CSTA (Computer Science Teachers Association) state/regional chapter officers,
board members, and active member-educators working at K-12 school districts in
your territory. Auto-queued by `scan_csta_chapters` when confidence=HIGH and a
district is named.

**Typical target:** State chapter president who also teaches CS at a specific
district. Entry point is the chapter relationship, not a cold district pitch.

## Theory of the pitch

CSTA chapter leaders are the most influential CS educators in their region.
They:
- Run conferences and workshops that other teachers attend
- Advise on state CS policy
- Get invited to district curriculum committees
- Are trusted by peers — one chapter president adopting a tool pulls 5-10
  other teachers with them

The pitch is **NOT** "buy CodeCombat." It's:
1. "Here's free stuff for your chapter members" (hour of code packs, conference
   sponsorship, raffle licenses)
2. "Here's a workshop I can give at your next chapter event" (product demo
   framed as PD)
3. "Would you be the chapter's liaison for an enterprise pilot?" (warm intro
   path into the district)

## Sequence structure (4 steps, ~14 days)

### Step 1 — Day 0 — Initial touch (CSTA chapter framed)

- **Subject:** `{chapter name} chapter — free resources from CodeCombat`
- **Tone:** Peer-to-peer, not sales. You're a CS curriculum person reaching out
  to another CS person who you respect.
- **Content:** Introduce yourself as Steven from CodeCombat. Mention their
  specific chapter role (we captured it in the signal). Offer one concrete
  free resource: conference swag + 25 free CodeCombat classroom licenses
  for chapter members to try. No pitch, no calendar link, no "let's hop on
  a call."
- **CTA:** Reply with an address and we'll ship the swag + trial license codes.

### Step 2 — Day 4 — Follow-up with conference angle

- **Subject:** `Re: {chapter name} chapter — free resources from CodeCombat`
  (threaded reply)
- **Content:** Mention you saw they're presenting at / attending [specific
  event]. Offer to underwrite a raffle giveaway at the event (1-year
  CodeCombat site license) OR give a 30-min workshop on "teaching real code
  without the usual pain" at the next chapter meeting.
- **CTA:** "Want me to line up the giveaway or the workshop?"

### Step 3 — Day 9 — District-level intro request

- **Subject:** `quick ask` (new subject to reset thread)
- **Content:** Assuming step 1 or 2 got a positive response: ask them to
  introduce you to their district's CS/curriculum lead. Frame it as "I'd love
  to show them what you're seeing in my stuff" rather than "can you forward
  me the decision-maker." Peer credibility is the ask.
- **CTA:** "Would you be willing to forward this to your district's CS lead
  with a one-liner?"

### Step 4 — Day 14 — Polite step-back

- **Subject:** `Re: {previous}`
- **Content:** If no response, one last low-pressure note. Offer to stay in
  touch via CSTA events, no ask.
- **CTA:** None. Just close the loop.

## Hard rules

- **NEVER** pitch pricing or tiers in steps 1-2. The goal is the chapter
  relationship first.
- **NEVER** use "I'd love to hop on a call" language. These are teachers, not
  procurement. Use async, concrete, specific.
- **NEVER** fabricate claims about their chapter or CodeCombat usage in other
  chapters. Only use verifiable facts.
- Every step references the *specific chapter* by name and the *specific role*
  (president, board member, etc) captured in the signal. No generic "CSTA
  member" templates.
- If they reply positively, break the sequence and go manual. CSTA partnerships
  are relationship plays — you can't automate the middle of them.

## Seasonal timing

- **Best months:** Sept-Oct (chapter year starts), Jan-Feb (conference
  planning), April-May (summer PD planning).
- **Avoid:** June-August (teachers on vacation), December (end-of-semester
  chaos), test weeks.

## Sponsorship tier reference (for step 2)

| Tier | Amount | Offer |
|---|---|---|
| Light | $0 | Swag + 25 trial licenses |
| Medium | $200-500 | + conference raffle license + 1 session sponsorship |
| Heavy | $1000+ | + keynote/workshop slot + chapter banner listing |

Default to Light unless Steven explicitly approves Medium/Heavy.

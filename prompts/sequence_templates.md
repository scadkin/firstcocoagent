# Email Sequence Templates

These archetypes guide Scout when building cold outreach sequences for Outreach.io.
Each archetype provides structural guidance — Scout personalizes using Steven's voice profile.

---

## ARCHETYPE: Cold Outreach — CS / STEM / CTE Directors & Coordinators

**Best for:** CS Director, STEM Director, CTE Director, Computer Science Coordinator, EdTech Director, Curriculum Director

**Core angle:** CodeCombat solves the #1 problem CS leaders face — getting students engaged and teachers confident. The suite is turnkey, standards-aligned, and proven at scale.

**Sequence structure:**
- Step 1 (Day 0): Short, specific cold intro. Reference their role + one real pain point (teacher prep, student engagement, standards alignment). One clear CTA: quick call or reply.
- Step 2 (Day 3): Value-add. Share a concrete data point or case study. Don't ask for anything — just be useful.
- Step 3 (Day 7): Social proof from a similar district or state. Name-drop a recognizable K-12 win if possible.
- Step 4 (Day 12): Try a different angle — product they haven't heard about (AI HackStack, Ozaria, AI League). Short.
- Step 5 (Day 18): The "not right timing?" step. Low pressure. Open the door for later.
- Step 6+ (optional): LinkedIn touch, referral ask, or re-engagement after 30 days.

**Tone:** Peer-to-peer. Not vendor-y. Steven knows K-12 CS deeply. Write like a colleague, not a sales rep.

## ARCHETYPE: Cold Outreach — Superintendents & Principals

**Best for:** Superintendent, Assistant Superintendent, Principal, Assistant Principal, CAO, Chief Academic Officer

**Core angle:** ROI and strategic fit. Supers care about: budget efficiency, measurable outcomes, teacher retention, community optics (especially CS/AI for workforce readiness).

**Sequence structure:**
- Step 1 (Day 0): 3 sentences max. Headline metric (e.g., "Districts using CodeCombat see 2x CS enrollment growth in year 2"). One ask.
- Step 2 (Day 4): Student outcome angle — engagement, retention, equity in CS.
- Step 3 (Day 9): Teacher angle — turnkey curriculum, low PD burden, built-in scaffolding.
- Step 4 (Day 15): Community / board optics angle — "What are you doing about AI readiness?"
- Step 5 (Day 22): Soft close / defer to team. "Is there someone on your curriculum team I should talk to?"

**Tone:** Respectful of their time. Lead with outcomes. No jargon.

## ARCHETYPE: Follow-Up After Demo Call

**Best for:** Any prospect who attended a product demo with Steven

**Core angle:** Momentum. Keep the energy from the demo alive. Make the next step obvious and easy.

**Sequence structure:**
- Step 1 (Day 1): Thank you + recap of what resonated in the call. Specific — reference something they said.
- Step 2 (Day 4): Address the top concern or question that came up. Provide an answer or resource.
- Step 3 (Day 8): Pilot proposal or pricing outline. Make it easy to say yes.
- Step 4 (Day 14): Check in. Are they still interested? Any internal blockers?
- Step 5 (Day 21): Last touch before archiving. "Still open to reconnecting?"

**Tone:** Warm, confident, not pushy. They already know Steven. This is nurture, not cold outreach.

---

## ARCHETYPE: Re-Engagement (Gone Silent)

**Best for:** Prospects who showed interest 3-6+ months ago but went quiet

**Core angle:** Things change. Budget cycles reset. New priorities emerge. Show up fresh with something new.

**Sequence structure:**
- Step 1 (Day 0): Reference the prior conversation. Acknowledge the gap. "A lot has changed — we launched X." Short.
- Step 2 (Day 5): Share a new product, case study, or market development relevant to them.
- Step 3 (Day 11): The "quick question" step — ask something specific about their district or goals.
- Step 4 (Day 18): Final check-in before archiving.

**Tone:** No guilt. No "just circling back." Make it relevant and current.

---

## ARCHETYPE: Referral Intro / Warm Outreach

**Best for:** Contacts who were referred by a mutual connection or who reached out inbound

**Core angle:** Lean on the warm signal. Get to value fast.

**Sequence structure:**
- Step 1 (Day 0): Reference the connection or inbound touchpoint. "So-and-so mentioned you're working on X." Show you've done homework.
- Step 2 (Day 3): Share the most relevant product/case study based on what you know about them.
- Step 3 (Day 8): Propose a specific next step — call, pilot, demo.
- Step 4 (Day 14): Soft follow-up if no response.

**Tone:** Warm, collegial. These leads are already warmer than cold — treat them that way.

## A/B TESTING GUIDANCE

When building a sequence, Scout can generate A/B variants for any step. Best candidates:
- **Subject lines:** Test curiosity vs. specific benefit vs. name-drop
- **Step 1 opener:** Test pain-first vs. outcome-first vs. question-first
- **CTA:** Test "15-minute call" vs. "reply with a question" vs. "would this be useful?"

Label variant A as the primary, variant B as the alternative. Steven picks which to test.
